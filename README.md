# Amazon-Product-Review-Dashboard

## Overview
This project analyzes Amazon product review data, capturing product identifiers, review metadata, user-generated feedback, ratings information, product categories, and pricing details including original price, discounted price, and discount percentages. The analysis aims to identify top-performing product categories, evaluate the impact of discounts on sales performance, and conduct exploratory analysis of ratings across categories and products to uncover key trends and patterns.
Dataset Description
The dataset contains Amazon product review data, including product and review identifiers, user feedback, ratings information, product categories, and pricing details such as original price, discounted price, and discount percentages.

## Source 
 
The dataset used in this project was sourced from Kaggle –https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/karkavelrajaj/amazon-sales-dataset

## Tools & Techniques Used (Excel)
  - Data cleaning and preparation (removing duplicates, handling missing values, standardizing formats)
  - Excel formulas and functions (VLOOKUP, IFS, AVERAGE etc)
  - Data transformation and creation of calculated columns
  - Pivot Tables and Pivot Charts for exploratory data analysis
  - Sorting, filtering, and conditional formatting for pattern identification
  - Data visualization using bar charts, line charts, and summary tables
  - Dashboard-style reporting for insight communication

## Key Insights

- Out of a total of 1,465 products, Electronics, Computers & Accessories, and Home & Kitchen have the highest product counts, with 526, 453, and 448 products respectively, while all other categories have fewer than 50 products each.
- Electronics receives the highest number of reviews, nearly double that of Computers & Accessories, and significantly higher than all other categories.
- Most products fall within the 20%–60% discount range.
- Very high discounts (above 80%) are uncommon across categories.
- Ratings are consistently high (4–5) across most product categories.
- Categories with fewer products still show strong average ratings.
- Average prices drop noticeably after discounts, indicating effective price reductions.
- Electronics and Home & Kitchen show the largest price differences before and after discount.
- Home Improvement and Computers & Accessories offer the highest average discount percentages.

## Recommendations:
- Prioritize high-performing categories such as Electronics, Computers & Accessories, and Home & Kitchen for inventory expansion and marketing investment, as they dominate product count and customer engagement. -- Leverage Electronics’ high review volume by promoting top-rated products and encouraging user reviews to further strengthen visibility and trust.
- Maintain discounts within the 20%–60% range, as this appears to be the most effective and widely adopted pricing strategy.
- Avoid excessive discounting above 80%, as it is rare and may negatively impact perceived product value and margins.
- Capitalize on consistently high ratings by highlighting customer satisfaction in marketing campaigns and product listings.
- Expand select high-rated, low-volume categories, as strong ratings indicate potential demand despite limited product availability.
•	Use targeted discount strategies for high-priced categories like Electronics and Home & Kitchen to drive conversions while managing profitability.
•	Apply higher discount strategies strategically in Home Improvement and Computers & Accessories to stimulate demand, while monitoring margin impact.
